KEEP YOUR HEART RATE UP — 6 TACTICS
Maintain intensity without sacrificing muscle stimulus
▼
⏱ Respect Rest CeilingsUse a stopwatch. Prescribed rest is a hard ceiling — not a floor. 90s means 90s max. Longer = lower metabolic demand = comfort zone = plateau.
🔄 Active Rest Between SetsBox step-ups, jumping jacks, battle rope slams, or dead hangs. Keeps HR above 110 BPM without fatiguing target muscles.
💧 Superset Non-Competing MusclesPair push/pull or upper/lower (e.g. bench → cable row). One muscle rests while the other works — density doubles, HR stays elevated.
🏁 8-Minute Finisher CircuitEnd each session: KB swings ×15 → Push-ups ×15 → Jump squats ×10. 3 rounds, 30s rest. Spikes lactate and triggers GH release.
☕ Pre-Workout Coffee TimingYour morning coffee is your pre-workout. Caffeine sustains HR 8–12% higher during training and enhances fat oxidation. Take 45–60 min before.
🌡️ Compound-First LoadingAlways lead with barbells/cables before isolation. Big multi-joint lifts demand more oxygen, drive HR higher, recruit more motor units.
🏃
CARDIO PROTOCOL — 2×/WEEK
Preserve muscle · burn fat · build work capacity
▼
💡 Why only 2×? At a caloric surplus, excess cardio competes with recovery and blunts muscle gain. Two targeted sessions — one LISS, one HIIT — optimizes cardiovascular fitness and body comp without costing gains.
Wednesday — LISS
Zone 2 Steady State · 30–35 min
Incline treadmill walk (3.5 mph, 8–10% grade) or cycling at 65–70% max HR. You can hold a conversation but it's not easy. Burns fat, boosts mitochondria, accelerates recovery.
Saturday — HIIT
Intervals · 20 Min Total
3 min warm-up. Then 8 rounds: 30s all-out (rowing, bike, or sprints) → 90s easy. Cool down 3 min. At 39, HIIT stimulates GH release and preserves muscle better than long steady cardio.
Sunday — Active Recovery
Optional · 20–30 min
Easy walk, light yoga, or foam rolling. Not mandatory — if sore or fatigued, skip it. This day is for meal prep and full recovery. Let the adaptation happen.
